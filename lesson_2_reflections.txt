What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?

  Git init, sets the specific directory as one that will be committed.

How is the staging area different form the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?

  The staging area is the intermediary area. It's what one is prepping to push to the repository. It allows you to segment and quarantine a clean slice of your code-base and make it ready to push

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?

  Make sure that you commit when you have one logical change.

What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?

  With branches, you can determine when to fork based on experiments, localizations ,and what not. Branches help to separate items and allow flexibility to experiment.

How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?

    Easy to trace. You can see where the forks are.

What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the digram the way we do?

  It combines the differences with the current master.

What are the pros and cons of Git's automatic merging vs. always doing merges manually?

  Pro: Simple and quick
  Con: Giving up control
